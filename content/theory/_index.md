The theoretical core of the artefact, in four sections:

- **Phase 1** — Linear-Gaussian POMDP, Schur complement, log-det.
  Closed-form derivative of mutual information with respect to a
  body parameter.
- **Phase 1.5** — Putting a measured body in. Warren's pi-numbers as
  the framing-paragraph object; the body-derivative stays in methods.
- **Phase 2** — Where the closed form breaks. Honest stopping.
- **Lemma** — The methods §1.5 type-match: why the framing-paragraph
  sign-direction claim is licensed by the methods section.

Each section is read linearly the first time. Code blocks are runnable
in your browser; see [Code](/code/) for the executable panels.

## Reader prerequisites

- Probability and information theory at definitional level: entropy,
  mutual information, KL divergence, Fisher information
- Basic linear algebra: block matrices, Schur complement, log-determinant
  identities (we derive what we need, but knowing the names helps)
- Willingness to follow Schur complement derivations carefully

If you are willing to work through `Phase 1` linearly, you have what
you need.
