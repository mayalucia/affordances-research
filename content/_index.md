## What this is

A research portal that develops one route to formalising affordance
theory — the idea, from Gibson and Warren, that whether an environment
"affords" an action depends on a relation between body and surround,
not on either alone.

The work is organised in three phases, each carrying a different kind
of empirical commitment:

- **Phase 1** — closed-form constructibility under a Linear-Gaussian
  POMDP. The body is parametric, the math is tractable, the result
  is verifiable three ways.
- **Phase 1.5** — putting a real, measured body in. Warren's
  pi-numbers are computed, not fitted. Framing-paragraph register
  stays in ecological-psychology terms; methods register carries the
  derivative.
- **Phase 2** — named, not built. Where the closed form breaks (when
  the body is estimated from action), we stop. We name the failure;
  we do not paper it over.

## Why a portal, not a paper

A paper would bury what is most worth showing: that the work has a
discipline, that the discipline ships with the artefact, and that
both can be inspected together.

The portal makes four things visible at once:

1. **Theory** — the closed-form derivations, written through.
2. **Code** — JAX panels that run in your browser. Three-verification
   triangle (analytical / autodiff / finite-difference) live.
3. **Method** — what we decided, what we deferred, what we don't
   build. Adversarial review absorbed into the work.
4. **Reading** — annotated bibliography. Every entry cite-or-strike
   checked. What each gets right, what we don't take from it.

## How to read

- Read **Theory** linearly the first time. Sections are interlocking;
  each ends with a checkpoint paragraph.
- Run the **Code** panels as you read. The three-verification triangle
  is meant to be inspected, not just read.
- **Method** is read in any order. Each section is a discrete piece
  of discipline.
- **Reading** is reference; consult while reading Theory.
